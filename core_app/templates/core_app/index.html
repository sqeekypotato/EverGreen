What is Evergreen Financial?
Lots of charts and graphs
Keep your bank happy.
With Evergreen Financial you don't have to give out your bank accounts password. There are quite a few banks out there that state giving out your password violates your terms of service and might and you might not be covered for fraud protection. On Evergreen Financial you will have to downloadyour transactions from your bank and upload them to Evergreen Financial. But this is a small price to pay for the added protection it provides.
Reports on tags that you select
Know what information you are supplying
Only the bare minimum of information is collected. When you upload your bank statements, the only the following information is kept from each transaction:
  • Date
  • Transaction description
  • Transaction Debit (if it took money out of your account)
  • Transaction Credit (if there was money put into your accout)
That is it! Your account balance is not even stored. You know what information you are giving up so you know how secure you are.
Set Budgets and See Historical Data on Tags that you choose
When you select a tag for drill-down information it will show you a specific historical chart just for that tag. Ever wonder if your insurance has gone up in the last year? Do you want to know how much you are making this year compared to last? One click provides these reports.
Simple Tagging of Transactions
When you tag a transaction Evergreen Financial will search through your other transactions to see if there is a match and also tag that record. This keeps the amount of work you need to do to a minimum. You can also create rules to automatically tag transactions. As well, every time you upload new transactions, it checks to see if it can tag any of the records before it asks you.

Filter Tags and Categories
You can view your transactions by Year or Month. There is also a separate chart for tags that do not have a fixed cost. Looking for ways to save money? You can set things like your mortgage and property taxes as a fixed cost and they will not appear in this chart. It helps bring into focus where you can cut back on your spending.
